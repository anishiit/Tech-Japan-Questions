### Technical Q&A (Prepare for these!)

> **Important Note:** Technical questions can vary significantly based on the company's tech stack and architecture. Be prepared to dive deep into the technologies you've listed on your resume and the ones used by the company.

#### Project-Specific Questions:

* **Why did you use this tech stack in your project?**
    * *(Justify your choices. Don't just list them. e.g., "We chose Next.js for SSR to get better SEO and initial load times...")*
* **What is your favorite tech stack?**
* **In which field do you want to work?** (e.g., Backend, Frontend, Full-Stack, AI)
* **What was the reason for making the project?** (Show initiative)
* **What challenges did you face, and how did you approach them?**
    * *(Have a specific story ready. "Hackathon crisis" is a great example. Use the STAR method: Situation, Task, Action, Result.)*
* **How do you start your project?**
    * *(This is a good place to mention Data Modeling. e.g., "I always start with planning and data modeling. I use tools like **ER diagrams** for structuring databases...")*

#### Database & Backend Concepts:

* **What is the difference between SQL and NoSQL databases?**
    * *(Discuss use cases, scalability, schema flexibility, ACID vs BASE properties)*
* **Difference between WebSocket & HTTPS request?**
    * *(Explain real-time communication vs request-response model, use cases for each)*
* **What is an API and what are the different types of APIs?**
    * *(REST, GraphQL, SOAP, gRPC - know when to use each)*

#### Networking & Systems:

* **Explain the seven layers of the OSI (Open Systems Interconnection) model.**
    * *(Physical, Data Link, Network, Transport, Session, Presentation, Application - know real-world examples)*

#### Programming Concepts:

* **Do you have an idea about OOPs (Object-Oriented Programming)?**
    * *(Encapsulation, Inheritance, Polymorphism, Abstraction - with practical examples)*
* **What is AOP (Aspect-Oriented Programming) in Java?**
    * *(Cross-cutting concerns, aspects, advice, join points, pointcuts)*

#### Frontend/React-Specific:

* **What is the use of spread operator in React?**
    * *(Demonstrate with examples: state updates in todo app, counter app, props spreading)*
* **Explain React life cycle methods.**
    * *(Class components vs Hooks: componentDidMount, useEffect, etc.)*

#### System Design:

* **Be prepared for system design questions such as:**
    * Design a URL shortener
    * Design a chat application
    * Design a notification system
    * Scalability, load balancing, caching strategies
    * Database sharding and replication

#### Most Important:

* **Explain and help me understand any of your best projects (‚≠ê VERY VERY IMPORTANT ‚≠ê)**
    * *(Be ready to explain architecture, tech decisions, challenges, scalability, and trade-offs in detail)*

#### Additional Preparation:

* **Many more questions will be based on:**
    * The company's specific tech stack
    * The architecture they follow (microservices, monolithic, serverless, etc.)
    * Technologies mentioned in your resume
    * Current industry trends and best practices

## 4. ü§ù Teamwork & Cultural Adaptability

### What is your strength compared to others?

> **Pro-Tip:** Be humble but confident. "Helping others" is a great-but-common answer. Back it up with a specific example.
>
> "My primary strength is development. I also genuinely enjoy helping my teammates and I like solving errors and bugs for my friends. For example, [Quick 1-sentence story about helping someone]."

### How do you work in teamwork and collaboration?

> "The **[Your Team Project]** was a hackathon-winning team project where we worked smoothly and collaborated well. We divided our respective tasks, completed them, and then moved on to helping each other in fixing bugs & errors.
>
> Additionally, as the **[Your Position of Responsibility]**, I have guided teams in building MVPs, ensuring seamless collaboration and innovation. I am also proficient with collaborative tools like Git, GitHub, and Discord."

### We work in the Japanese language. How will you handle it?

> **Pro-Tip:** This is a crucial test of your commitment. Show you've *already started* and are eager to learn more.
>
> "I am currently learning Japanese through **[Your Learning Method, e.g., Duolingo/a course]** and have already picked up some basic words like **[a few basic words you know]**. I am genuinely interested in learning more of the language and improving my proficiency.
>
> To ensure smooth communication and collaboration, I will actively practice, immerse myself in the language, and adapt quickly. I am confident that with consistent effort, I will be able to work effectively in a Japanese-speaking environment while giving my 100% to the company."

### What do you know about Japanese work culture?

> "I understand that Japanese work culture emphasizes respect, hierarchy, punctuality, and teamwork. The concept of **"Ho-Ren-So"** (reporting, communication, and consultation) is fundamental. I am adaptable and open to learning new work styles, and I am willing to embrace these principles to contribute effectively."

### How do you handle constructive criticism from a senior or manager?

> "I welcome constructive feedback as an opportunity to improve. In Japanese work culture, I understand that feedback is given to ensure collective success, and I would take it positively to enhance my skills and work quality."

### When do you feel frustrated?

> "I feel frustrated when deadlines are at risk. I am disciplined and prefer to do work on a deadline. When this happens, I usually take a short break to clear my head, then get back to the problem to try to minimize any loss and find a solution."
